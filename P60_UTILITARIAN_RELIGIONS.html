This is particularly true of Unitarian Universalism; a rather open-ended community of people whose personal epistemological and ethical beliefs can differ quite radically since all Unitarian Universalists presumably believe that (a) each and every human being deserves unconditional salvation by Jesus Christ and (b) religion is meant to be open-ended and individualistic rather than closed off to new possibilities and conformist.
